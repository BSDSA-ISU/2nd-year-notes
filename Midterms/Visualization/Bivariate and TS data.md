# 📊 Bivariate & Time Series Data Visualization Reviewer

## 🧠 What is Bivariate Data?

Bivariate data = **data involving TWO variables**

* “Bi” = two
* You’re studying relationships, not lonely single-variable behavior anymore

### 🎯 Goal:

* Find relationships
* See patterns between variables
* Predict one variable using another

Example pairs:

* Study hours ↔ Exam score
* Height ↔ Weight
* Temperature ↔ Ice cream sales

---

## 📈 Main Bivariate Visualization

### 1. 📍 Scatter Plot

The absolute king of bivariate data.

Each point = one pair (x, y)

Example:

* x = study hours
* y = exam score

```text id="x7k2p1"
Score
100 |        •
 90 |      •
 80 |    •
 70 |  •
 60 | •
     ----------------
       Hours studied
```

### 📌 What to look for:

* Positive correlation → both increase together 📈
* Negative correlation → one goes up, other goes down 📉
* No correlation → chaos, basically life

---

### 2. 📊 Line of Best Fit (Trend Line)

A line drawn through scatter plot data.

Purpose:

* Summarize trend
* Predict values

```text id="b8m2q4"
y
|
|      •
|    •   •
|  •  -----
| •
|________________ x
```

Yes, it’s basically “organized guessing with math permission.”

---

### 3. 📦 Correlation Coefficient (r)

Not a graph, but it controls your life here.

* r = +1 → perfect positive relationship
* r = -1 → perfect negative relationship
* r = 0 → no relationship (just vibes)

---

## 🌊 What is Time Series Data?

Time series = **data collected over time**

Example:

* Daily temperature
* Monthly sales
* Website traffic per hour
* Your motivation level across school weeks (spoiler: downward trend)

### 🎯 Goal:

* Identify trends over time
* Detect seasonal patterns
* Forecast future values (aka educated guessing)

---

## 📈 Main Time Series Visualization

### 1. 📉 Line Graph

The ONLY graph that matters here.

```text id="t9v4x2"
Value
|
|        •
|      •
|    •
|  •
|•
|________________ Time
```

### 📌 What to look for:

* Trend → overall direction (up/down/stable)
* Seasonality → repeating patterns (like holidays or exam stress cycles)
* Noise → random fluctuations (aka life being annoying)

---

## 📊 Key Differences (Important)

| Feature    | Bivariate     | Time Series       |
| ---------- | ------------- | ----------------- |
| Variables  | 2 variables   | 1 variable + time |
| X-axis     | Any variable  | Always time       |
| Purpose    | Relationships | Change over time  |
| Main graph | Scatter plot  | Line graph        |

---

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Exam traps, because of course)

* Thinking correlation = causation ❌
  (“Ice cream causes crime” level logic)

* Using scatter plot for time series ❌

* Ignoring time order in data ❌

* Confusing trend with random noise ❌

* Over-interpreting weak correlation ❌

---

## 🧠 Quick Mental Cheatsheet

* Scatter plot → “Do these two things relate?”
* Line of best fit → “What’s the trend pretending to be?”
* Time series → “How does this change over time?”
* Line graph → “Let’s watch data suffer through time”

| Topic       | Tool            | Purpose                          |

## 🧩 Final Thought

Bivariate data asks:

> “Do these two things care about each other?”

Time series asks:

> “How badly is this thing changing over time?”

Statistics, in general, is just structured observation pretending it’s not emotional damage.
